**Content Map for Steve Jobs' 2005 Stanford Commencement Address**

1. **[00:00:00-00:00:22] Introduction**
   - **Title/Topic:** Opening Remarks
   - **Description:** Steve Jobs expresses his honor to speak at Stanford's commencement and acknowledges his lack of a college graduation.
   - **Key Moment:** "I never graduated from college and this is the closest I've ever gotten to a college graduation."

2. **[00:00:22-00:05:47] Story One: Connecting the Dots**
   - **Title/Topic:** Dropping Out of College
   - **Description:** Jobs shares his experience of dropping out of Reed College and how following his curiosity led to valuable lessons, particularly in calligraphy, which influenced the design of the Macintosh.
   - **Key Moment:** "You can't connect the dots looking forward; you can only connect them looking backwards."

3. **[00:05:47-00:09:52] Story Two: Love and Loss**
   - **Title/Topic:** The Experience of Being Fired
   - **Description:** Jobs recounts starting Apple, achieving success, and then being fired. He reflects on how this setback led to a creative resurgence, founding NeXT and Pixar, and ultimately returning to Apple.
   - **Key Moment:** "Sometimes life hits you in the head with a brick. Don't lose faith."

4. **[00:09:52-00:13:06] Story Three: Death**
   - **Title/Topic:** Embracing Mortality
   - **Description:** Jobs discusses a pivotal moment when he was diagnosed with cancer, how it shaped his perspective on life choices, and the importance of following one's heart.
   - **Key Moment:** "Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life."

5. **[00:13:06-00:14:28] Conclusion**
   - **Title/Topic:** Final Thoughts
   - **Description:** Jobs shares a significant message from The Whole Earth Catalog, encouraging graduates to "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish."
   - **Key Moment:** "Stay Hungry. Stay Foolish. And I have always wished that for myself." 

6. **[00:14:28-00:15:01] Closing Remarks**
   - **Title/Topic:** Thank You and Farewell
   - **Description:** Jobs concludes his speech with gratitude to the audience.
   - **Key Moment:** "Thank you all very much."
